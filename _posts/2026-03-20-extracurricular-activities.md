University isn't just about grades and textbooks. Some of the most important things I learned at Foreign Trade University happened outside the lecture halls -- on the volleyball court and in the meeting rooms of the Logistics Studying Club.

## Spiking My Way Through University

I joined the volleyball team in my first year, mostly because I needed an excuse to exercise and make friends. I didn't expect it to become one of the defining experiences of my time at FTU.

Over four years of FTU Sport Games (2018--2022), our team collected **a Bronze medal (2018), a Gold medal (2019), and a Silver medal (2020)**. Each tournament taught me something different:

- **2018 (Bronze)**: We were the underdogs, still figuring out how to play together. Winning bronze felt like a triumph.
- **2019 (Gold)**: Everything clicked. We trained harder, communicated better, and played with a confidence we hadn't had before. Standing on top of the podium with my teammates is still one of my favorite university memories.
- **2020 (Silver)**: So close to defending our title. The loss stung, but it reminded me that you can do everything right and still come up short -- and that's okay.

## The Logistics Studying Club

While volleyball was about physical energy and teamwork, the **Logistics Studying Club** fed my intellectual curiosity. From 2018 to 2022, I was an active member, participating in academic workshops and discussions on supply chain management.

The club organized events like **SCMission**, a supply chain competition that brought together students and industry professionals. Being part of the organizing team for the 2019 Gala Night was a crash course in event management -- coordinating with sponsors, managing logistics (how fitting), and making sure everything ran smoothly on the big day.

In 2019, I also had the opportunity to **lead the Spring Volunteer Campaign**, which taught me a different kind of leadership. Organizing volunteers, coordinating with local communities, and seeing the direct impact of our work gave me a sense of purpose beyond career ambitions.

## Finding Balance

Looking back, these extracurricular activities weren't distractions from my studies -- they were essential complements to them. Volleyball taught me discipline and teamwork. The Logistics Club connected my classroom knowledge to the real world. Volunteering reminded me why any of it matters.

If I could give one piece of advice to fellow students: don't just study. Join a team. Volunteer for something. Put yourself in situations where the outcome isn't guaranteed. Those are the experiences that shape you -- not the grades on your transcript.
