Some journeys you plan carefully. Others just unfold, one decision at a time. My academic path from a small coastal province in Vietnam to a Master's program in France has been a bit of both.

## Where It All Started

I grew up in Binh Dinh, a province in central Vietnam known more for its beaches and martial arts heritage than for academia. But I was lucky to attend **Le Quy Don High School for the Gifted**, where I specialized in English. Those three years (2015--2018) gave me a solid foundation -- I graduated with an average of 8.7/10 -- but more importantly, they opened a window to the world beyond Vietnam.

## Ho Chi Minh City: The Undergraduate Years

After high school, I moved to Ho Chi Minh City to study **International Business Economics** at **Foreign Trade University** (2018--2022). It was my first time living in a big city, and the contrast with Binh Dinh was striking -- everything moved faster, the competition was fiercer, and the opportunities were endless.

At FTU, I discovered my passion for economics and data. Courses in Econometrics, International Trade Policy, and Logistics gave me the analytical toolkit that would shape my career interests. I graduated with a GPA of 3.3/4.0, but the numbers don't capture the full story -- the late-night study sessions, the friendships forged over group projects, and the slow realization that I wanted to go deeper into economics.

## A Detour Through Industry

Before heading to France, I spent a year at **Hyosung Corporation** as a Purchasing Executive. Managing procurement for 300+ types of machine components, processing 200+ purchase orders monthly, and negotiating with suppliers gave me a real-world perspective that no classroom could. It also confirmed something I already suspected: I wanted to understand the *why* behind economic decisions, not just execute them.

## Learning French in Rennes

The transition to France started with language. In 2024, I enrolled at **University Rennes 2** to study French, starting from B1 and working up to B2. Learning a new language as an adult is humbling -- you feel like a child again, fumbling for words, misunderstanding jokes, and celebrating small victories like ordering coffee without switching to English.

I earned my University Diplomas in French Studies with scores of 17.219/20 (B1) and 17.124/20 (B2), which I'm quite proud of. Rennes itself was a lovely introduction to France -- a student-friendly city with beautiful parks, great crepes, and just enough rain to make you appreciate the sunny days.

## Orleans: The Current Chapter

Now I'm in **Orleans**, pursuing a **Master in International Economics and Sustainable Development**. And honestly? It's been the most intellectually stimulating period of my life. Panel Econometrics, Time Series, Development Economics, Environmental Economics -- every course feels like a piece of a larger puzzle I'm still assembling.

The highlight so far: **ranking 1st out of 15 students** in my first semester, with an average of 15.39/20. It's a small cohort, which means close relationships with professors and classmates. We come from all over the world -- France, Africa, Asia, Latin America -- and the diversity of perspectives makes every classroom discussion richer.

## What's Next?

Looking ahead, I want to apply my analytical skills to practical problems -- particularly in sustainability and supply chain management. The combination of econometrics, data analysis, and real-world business experience feels like a unique toolkit, and I'm excited to see where it leads.

But if there's one thing this journey has taught me, it's that the best opportunities are often the ones you don't see coming. From Binh Dinh to Ho Chi Minh City to Rennes to Orleans -- each step felt uncertain at the time, but looking back, they all make sense.

The journey continues.
